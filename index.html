Problem

The accuracy gap in early drug discovery

Classical docking and force fields mis-rank ligands because they ignore essential electronic effects. Quantum methods are accurate but too slow for million-scale libraries.

Unmet need

Computational and medicinal chemists require reliable binding energies during hit-to-lead. Existing physics-based tools lack electronic fidelity, causing costly experimental failures and low hit rates.

Target users: >10,000 chemists in pharma, mid-stage and early biotechs who depend on predictive modeling.

Quantum Click’s advantage

Quantum-level accuracy with the speed to re-rank millions of docked poses. Unique ML+QM hybrid captures electron density explicitly, delivering dependable rank order while fitting standard pharma workflows.

Technology

Hybrid architecture for accuracy and throughput

Symmetry-aware ML electron densities + orbital-free DFT interaction energies + DynamicBind sampling.

Symmetry-aware ML density

Split-charge Equilibrium with parametrized charges (SQE+qp) produces near-DFT electron distributions suitable for large systems.

Orbital-free DFT evaluator

Proprietary interaction-energy method (patent pending) approximates QM accuracy at throughput comparable to classical screening.

Cloud-scale delivery

AWS-native orchestration enables isolated VPC deployments, elastic compute, and API/GUI access for diverse teams.

Performance & workflow

Drug-centered small-cell approach cuts evaluation from 135s to 6.9s for ligands and from 144s to <1s for complexes—without loss of accuracy. Supports JSON/CSV outputs for LIMS/ELN integration.

Benchmarks and partner expectations

Benchmarking

  • Mean deviation: 1.2 kcal/mol overall vs DFT on S22; 1.6 kcal/mol for H-bonded systems

Throughput
